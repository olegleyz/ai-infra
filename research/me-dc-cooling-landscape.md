## 1. Major Middle East Data Center Operators & Cooling Approaches

### Khazna Data Centers (UAE)
- **What:** Largest UAE DC operator (~70% market share), expanded from 28 MW (2020) to 500+ MW (2025). Investing in direct liquid cooling and immersion cooling for next-gen AI chips rated for 45C+ ambient temperatures.
- **Where:** Abu Dhabi, Dubai, Ajman (100 MW facility announced Oct 2024)
- **Status:** Operational and expanding. First phase of Ajman AI-optimized facility expected online 2026.
- **Notable:** Switched all generators to biofuel-powered (July 2024), replacing diesel. Partnered with Masdar for 7 MWp solar at Abu Dhabi 6 facility.
- Sources: [Rest of World](https://restofworld.org/2025/khazna-data-center-uae/), [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/khazna-announces-100mw-ajman-data-center-uae/)

### Submer + Zero Two (UAE)
- **What:** 300+ MW of single-phase immersion cooling deployed in Abu Dhabi. Zero-water design using dielectric fluid instead of water. Zero Two (joint venture of G42 and ADNOC) can commission 100MW+ sites within weeks.
- **Where:** Abu Dhabi
- **Status:** Operational (2024-2025). Largest immersion cooling deployment in the Middle East.
- Sources: [Submer](https://submer.com/blog/press/zero-two-partnership-to-accelerate-uaes-ai-growth/)

### Gulf Data Hub (UAE)
- **What:** Expanding across UAE with long-term plans for neighboring markets.
- **Where:** UAE
- **Status:** Operational, expanding.
- Source: [S&P Global](https://www.spglobal.com/market-intelligence/en/news-insights/research/2025/10/gulf-data-hub-invests-in-further-expansion-in-uae-long-term-plans-for-neighboring-markets)

### Equinix (Oman, UAE, Saudi Arabia)
- **What:** Opened SN1 in Salalah, Oman (Nov 2024) in partnership with Omantel. Plans for $1B Saudi data center. Sixth facility in the region.
- **Where:** Oman, UAE, Saudi Arabia
- **Status:** SN1 operational. Saudi facility planned.
- Sources: [Equinix/Omantel](https://www.whtop.com/news/32707-equinix-opens-data-center-in-oman-boosting-connectivity-with-omantel), [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/equinix-plans-1bn-saudi-data-center/)

### AWS, Microsoft, Oracle (Saudi Arabia)

- **Where:** Saudi Arabia (Riyadh, Jeddah, Eastern Province)

### Pure Data Centres (UAE)

### ICS Arabia / Desert Dragon (Saudi Arabia)

- **Where:** Riyadh

### HUMAIN Programme (Saudi Arabia)
- **What:** National AI infrastructure programme pushing rack densities above 100 kW, driving adoption of immersion and two-phase liquid cooling at scale.

## 2. Liquid Immersion Cooling in Hot Climates

### Submer + Zero Two (Abu Dhabi) -- see above
- 300+ MW deployed. Single-phase, zero-water. Largest ME immersion deployment.
- **Status:** Operational.

### GRC (Green Revolution Cooling)
- **What:** Launched "Next-Gen Immersion Cooled Data Centers" for MEA (Oct 2023) in collaboration with Dell Technologies and DCV Industries. In Jan 2025, launched automated coolant lifecycle management platform with real-time purity monitoring.
- **Where:** Middle East & Africa region
- **Status:** Product available, specific ME deployment details limited.
- Source: [Data Center Frontier](https://www.datacenterfrontier.com/cooling/article/55130995/turn-up-the-volume-data-center-liquid-immersion-cooling-advancements-so-far-in-2024)

### Submer Two-Phase Immersion (Global)
- **What:** In March 2025, introduced next-gen two-phase immersion tank for ultra-high-density AI workloads with advanced vapor management, leak detection, and improved heat exchange.
- **Status:** Product launch. ME deployment likely via Zero Two partnership.

### Industry Context
- Liquid immersion cooling can yield 40% energy savings (per UAE Ministry of Technology).
- Global hyperscale adoption of liquid cooling expected to grow 142% between 2025-2030.
- Saudi HUMAIN programme and UAE AI corridor pushing rack densities above 100 kW, requiring immersion/two-phase cooling.

## 3. Seawater / Ocean Cooling

### DataVolt + NEOM Oxagon (Saudi Arabia)
- **What:** 1.5 GW net-zero AI factory campus at Oxagon on the Red Sea coast. Will use Red Sea seawater for cooling -- no freshwater consumption. Powered entirely by renewable energy.
- **Where:** NEOM Oxagon, Red Sea coast, Saudi Arabia
- **Status:** Agreement signed February 2025. Phase 1 ($5B) expected operational by 2028.
- Sources: [NEOM](https://www.neom.com/en-us/newsroom/datavolt-signs-agreement-with-neom), [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/datavolt-plans-15gw-data-center-campus-in-neoms-oxagon/)

### Nautilus Data Technologies (Global, not yet ME)
- **What:** Floating barge-based data centers using raw water cooling at 4,500 gal/min. PUE of 1.15. Second unit installed Feb 2025. Expanding to California and Southeast Asia.
- **Where:** Stockton, California (operational). Expansion planned.
- **Status:** Operational and scaling. No ME deployment announced yet, but the model is directly applicable to Gulf coastal sites.
- Source: [Nautilus](https://nautilusdt.com/)

### Microsoft Project Natick (Reference)
- **What:** Underwater data center off Scotland's Orkney Islands (2020). Achieved 80% energy efficiency -- nearly double industry average. Eliminated water cooling costs entirely.
- **Status:** Completed research project. Validated the concept.

### Caspian Sea Feasibility Study
- **What:** Academic feasibility study on seawater cooling for data centers using Caspian Sea water.
- **Status:** Published research.
- Source: [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2213138821005397)

### Gulf Water Challenge Context
- Saudi DC water consumption was 15 billion liters in 2024, projected to rise dramatically.
- MEA DC water consumption projected to climb from 119.3B liters (2025) to 426.3B liters (2030).
- Gulf seawater temperatures (30-35C in summer) are warmer than ideal, limiting direct seawater cooling efficiency compared to Nordic waters, but still viable for pre-cooling loops.
- Sources: [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2612606/saudi-arabia), [Rest of World](https://restofworld.org/2025/gulf-ai-water-crisis/)

## 4. Underground / Cave Data Centers

### No Known ME Underground DC Projects

There are no identified underground or cave-based data center projects in the Middle East as of early 2026.

### Oracle Underground DC (Israel)
- **What:** Subterranean server hall in Bet Shemesh, east of Jerusalem. 16 MW capacity. Designed to survive a direct missile attack.
- **Status:** Expected online Q2 2025.
- Source: [DCD](https://www.datacenterdynamics.com/en/news/oracles-data-center-israel-safra-catz/)

### MedOne (Israel)
- **What:** 320,000+ sq ft of underground data center space across multiple sites.
- **Status:** Operational.

### Global References
- **Lefdal Mine (Norway):** Converted mine, natural cooling from mountain rock and fjord water. Operational.
- **Bahnhof Pionen (Sweden):** Cold War-era nuclear bunker under granite. Operational.
- **Iron Mountain (Pennsylvania, USA):** Limestone mine converted to data center. Operational.

### ME Feasibility Challenges
- Underground construction costs $2,000+/sq ft (2x above-ground in US, potentially more in ME).
- 3+ year build time vs 1.5-3 years above-ground.
- Complicates maintenance, expansion, and cooling infrastructure.
- ME geology (largely sedimentary rock, sand) less favorable than Nordic granite/limestone.

## 5. District Cooling Integration

### Empower (Dubai) -- World's Largest District Cooling Provider
- **What:** 87 district cooling plants, responsible for 80%+ of Dubai's district cooling capacity. Business Bay project holds Guinness record at 241,272 RT (October 2024). Total Business Bay capacity: 451,540 RT across 9 plants.
- **Where:** Dubai
- **Status:** Operational and expanding. New 47,000 RT plant announced for Dubai Science Park.
- Sources: [Gulf Business](https://gulfbusiness.com/empower-to-build-new-47000-rt-district-cooling-plant-at-dubai-science-park/), [Empower](https://www.mediaoffice.ae/en/news/2024/may/06-05/empower-announces-q1-2024-results)

### Empower + Moro Hub (Dubai)
- **What:** Empower hosts its IT workloads at Moro's Green Data Center (Moro is a DEWA subsidiary). Received Green Certificate. Reduced carbon emissions by ~35,371 kg CO2e during hosting period.
- **Status:** Operational. This represents a DC using district cooling indirectly -- Empower as customer, not provider to DCs.
- **Gap:** No confirmed examples of a commercial data center being cooled *by* district cooling infrastructure were found. The integration appears to be nascent despite the obvious opportunity.

### District Cooling Context
- Gulf states operate the world's largest district cooling systems.
- Dubai alone has district cooling capacity exceeding 2 million RT.
- Empower uses AI-driven SCADA systems and thermal energy storage to reduce peak loads.
- The technology is proven at massive scale for buildings but has not been widely documented as a cooling source for data centers specifically.

## 7. Radiative Cooling Technology

### SkyCool Systems
- **What:** Rooftop panels that use radiative sky cooling to reject heat to space. Demonstrated 10-20% efficiency improvements on commercial cooling systems.
- **Where:** Primarily US deployments (California pilots, US military bases).
- **Status:** No confirmed Middle East deployments or pilots found.
- Source: [SkyCool Systems](https://www.skycoolsystems.com/)

### i2Cool (Hong Kong)
- **What:** Released PDRC (Passive Daytime Radiative Cooling) system designed specifically for data centers.
- **Status:** Product available. No ME deployment confirmed.

### Research Context
- A 2025 study in *Journal of Thermal Science* analyzed radiative cooling specifically for data center cooling and energy efficiency.
- Industry analysts note the Middle East as a "significant future growth opportunity" for PDRC technology.
- **Challenge for ME:** Radiative cooling works best in dry climates with clear skies (good for Gulf). However, high ambient temperatures and dust accumulation on panels are practical concerns.
- Source: [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11630-025-2146-x)

## 8. Traditional Architecture-Inspired Approaches

### Masdar City Wind Tower (Abu Dhabi)
- **What:** A modern interpretation of the traditional barjeel (wind tower). Giant Teflon tube in a metal frame captures prevailing winds, channels them through mist jets into a courtyard. Computer-controlled louvers with weather station input. Reduces felt temperature by 5-10C.
- **Where:** Masdar City, Abu Dhabi
- **Status:** Operational (built as part of Masdar Institute by Foster + Partners).
- **DC Application:** Not applied to data centers. The principle (passive convection + evaporative cooling) works for occupied spaces but would struggle with the heat density of server racks.
- Sources: [Earth Bound Report](https://earthbound.report/2018/08/24/building-of-the-week-masdars-wind-tower/), [Foster + Partners](https://www.fosterandpartners.com/projects/masdar-institute)

### Traditional Qanat + Wind Tower Systems
- **What:** Historical Persian/Gulf architecture paired wind towers with underground water channels (qanats). Hot air drawn down into tunnels, cooled by earth contact and water evaporation.
- **Modern DC Relevance:** The principle of earth-contact cooling (stable underground temperatures ~25C in Gulf) could inform underground cable routing or buried heat rejection systems, but no DC application has been documented.

### Masdar City Broader Approach
- **What:** The entire city uses "passive" design -- oriented streets to channel wind, thermal mass, shading, radiant cooling in buildings, "coolth" recovery systems.
- **Status:** Operational. Demonstrates principles but hasn't been translated to DC design.
- Source: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2014/01/02/world/meast/dubai-can-wind-power-take-the-heat)

### Gap Assessment
- No data center anywhere has been documented as incorporating wind tower, thermal mass, or earth-sheltering principles. The heat densities involved (kW per rack vs. watts per square meter in buildings) make direct translation impractical. However, these techniques could augment peripheral cooling (lobbies, office spaces, outdoor heat rejection zones).

## 10. Workload Shifting / Computational Strategies

### No ME-Specific Implementations Found

No documented cases of Middle East data center operators implementing time-of-day workload shifting were identified. However, the concept is well-studied globally:

### Global Research & Practice
- **MIT Sloan (2024):** Flexible data centers that shift batch processing to nighttime hours and throttle compute during peaks can reduce energy costs by up to 30%.
- **NREL:** Aggressive peak shaving with battery systems saved up to 30% of energy costs.
- **Texas grid study:** Flexible workloads in wind/solar-heavy grids reduced emissions by up to 40%.
- **Spatio-temporal shifting:** Research on shifting workloads across both time and geography to match renewable availability.
- Sources: [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/flexible-data-centers-can-reduce-costs-if-not-emissions), [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666792424000404)

### ME Opportunity
- Gulf states have massive solar generation that peaks midday, while cooling loads also peak midday. Shifting deferrable compute workloads to nighttime (when cooling is cheaper but solar is unavailable) involves a tradeoff.
- The more promising strategy for ME may be geographic shifting -- routing deferrable workloads to cooler-climate DCs during Gulf daytime peaks.

## 11. Advanced Coolants & Heat Exchangers

### Dielectric Fluids in ME
- **Submer/Zero Two deployment (Abu Dhabi):** Uses proprietary single-phase dielectric fluid for 300+ MW of immersion cooling. Zero-water design.
- **Status:** Operational.

### Coolant Technology Landscape
- **Single-phase fluids:** Synthetic esters, engineered hydrocarbons. Stable, safe, biodegradable options available.
- **Two-phase fluids:** Fluorocarbons that boil at chip surface, providing superior heat transfer. Higher cost. Submer's 2025 two-phase tank targets ultra-high-density AI.
- **Key specs for ME:** Flashpoint >150C, autoignition >300C, dielectric strength >6 kV/mm. Bio-based options have zero GWP.
- **GRC platform (2025):** Automated coolant lifecycle management with real-time purity monitoring -- addresses the practical challenge of maintaining fluid quality in harsh environments.

### Heat Exchanger Innovations
- **Dry coolers:** Increasingly used in ME to reject heat to air without water evaporation. Less efficient than wet cooling but eliminates water consumption.
- **Hybrid systems:** Combine dry cooling with adiabatic (evaporative) assist during peak temperatures. Reduce water use by 80-90% compared to traditional cooling towers.

## 12. Waste Heat Reuse

### DC Waste Heat for Desalination (Concept/Pilot Stage)
- **What:** Using data center waste heat to power multi-effect distillation (MED) thermal desalination. A "regenerative data center" concept introduced by Schneider Electric (2022) envisions DCs that produce their own freshwater.
- **Where:** Conceptual for ME. NEOM and Masdar cited as potential pilot locations.
- **Status:** Feasibility studies and academic research. No operational deployment in ME found.
- **Potential:** If even a small portion of Gulf DC capacity adopted heat-to-desalination, output could reach hundreds of thousands of cubic meters/day of freshwater.
- **Challenge:** Technical integration of two complex systems requires cutting-edge engineering. DC waste heat temperatures (30-60C) are on the low end for efficient thermal desalination.
- Sources: [Arab News](https://www.arabnews.com/node/2612606/saudi-arabia), [Lund University thesis](https://lup.lub.lu.se/student-papers/record/9198095/file/9198100.pdf)

### Heat Reuse Global Context
- In Europe, DC waste heat is increasingly piped to district heating networks (e.g., Stockholm, Helsinki, Dublin).
- In the Gulf, there is no heating demand, so desalination is the most logical heat sink.
- Aquaculture (warm water fish farming) and industrial process pre-heating are other theoretical uses.

### Closed-Loop Water Systems
- **What:** Gulf operators increasingly using closed-loop cooling, treated wastewater, greywater, and condensate recovery instead of potable freshwater.
- **Status:** Becoming standard practice across new ME facilities.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Immersion cooling is the dominant innovation in ME today.** The Submer/Zero Two deployment in Abu Dhabi (300+ MW) is the most significant real-world cooling innovation in the region. It eliminates water consumption entirely.

2. **Seawater cooling is the big bet for 2028+.** The DataVolt/NEOM Oxagon project (1.5 GW, Red Sea seawater) is the most ambitious planned facility. If it delivers, it becomes a proof point for coastal hot-climate DCs globally.

3. **The Line pivot to DC hub is a major development.** Saudi Arabia's $50B+ investment in NEOM infrastructure may find its highest-value use as AI data center land rather than a residential mega-city.

4. **Several obvious opportunities remain unexploited:**
   - District cooling integration (Empower's massive infrastructure is steps away from Dubai DCs)
   - Waste heat for desalination (technically feasible, no one has built it yet)
   - Radiative cooling (ideal climate conditions, no pilots)
   - Traditional architecture principles (wind towers, earth contact, thermal mass -- untested for DCs)

5. **Water is the critical constraint.** Gulf DC water consumption is projected to quadruple by 2030. Zero-water cooling (immersion, dry coolers) is not optional -- it's existential for the region's DC growth.
